FREQUENCY COORDINATION

Wireless Frequency Management

Budget System (Shure BLX + PSM 300)

  • Both operate in similar UHF range (518-542 MHz)
  • Total channels needed: 8 (6 mics + 2 IEMs)
  • BLX88 x 3 = 6 channels
  • P3T x 2 = 2 channels
  • Available bandwidth: 24 MHz
  • Recommended: Use QuickScan on all units
  • Coordination: Manual preset selection

Mid-Range System (All Sennheiser EW)

  • EW-D: 506-530 MHz (24 MHz bandwidth)
  • EW IEM G4: 516-558 MHz (42 MHz bandwidth)
  • Overlap: 516-530 MHz (14 MHz available for coordination)
  • Total channels: 8 (6 mics + 2 IEMs)
  • Equidistant frequency grid makes coordination easy
  • Use auto-scan function on receivers
  • High-Density mode: Up to 90 channels possible
  • BEST COORDINATION - Same brand, overlapping bands

Premium System (All Shure ULX-D + PSM 1000)

  • Both systems use same frequency bands
  • Total channels: 8 (6 mics + 2 IEMs)
  • High-Density mode: 60+ channels per band
  • Use Wireless Workbench software for coordination
  • AES-256 encryption available
  • Network monitoring and control
  • EASIEST COORDINATION - Same manufacturer, advanced software

Syria Frequency Considerations

  • Verify legal UHF bands with Syrian telecom authority
  • Common legal bands: 518-566 MHz, 606-614 MHz
  • Wireless microphone license may be required
  • Coordinate with local TV broadcast channels
  • Consider interference from 4G/5G mobile networks
