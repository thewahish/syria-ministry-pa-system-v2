Wireless Frequency Verification Guide for Syria

How to Verify Legal UHF Frequencies for Wireless Microphones

Overview

Before purchasing wireless microphone equipment for Syria, you MUST verify the legal UHF frequency bands with the Syrian telecommunications regulatory authority. Using unauthorized frequencies is illegal and can result in:

  • Equipment confiscation
  • Fines
  • Interference with other critical communications
  • Project delays

Syrian Regulatory Authority

Official Authority: Syrian Telecommunication & Post Regulatory Authority (SY-TPRA)

Previously known as: Syrian Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (SYTRA) Name changed: 2019 Official Website:

  • https://sytpra.gov.sy/ (Regulatory authority site)

HOW TO VERIFY FREQUENCIES - Step by Step

Method 1: Direct Contact with SY-TPRA (RECOMMENDED)

Step 1: Prepare Your Inquiry

Create a formal letter (in English and Arabic if possible) requesting:

`

Subject: Request for Wireless Microphone Frequency Authorization

Dear SY-TPRA,

We are [Your Organization/Ministry Name] and require information about

legal UHF frequency bands for professional wireless microphone systems

for use in Syria.

Equipment Details:

  • Application: Professional PA system for government use
  • Equipment Type: UHF wireless microphones and in-ear monitors
  • Number of Channels: 8 channels (6 microphones + 2 IEMs)
  • Proposed Frequency Range: 500-600 MHz UHF band

We request:

  1. Permitted UHF frequency bands for wireless microphones
  2. Required power levels (typically 10-50 mW for microphones)
  3. License requirements and application process
  4. Any technical standards or certifications required
  5. Application forms (if applicable)

Contact Information:

[Your Name]

[Your Title]

[Organization]

[Phone Number]

[Email Address]

TYPICAL UHF FREQUENCY BANDS (REFERENCE ONLY)

Note: These are TYPICAL frequencies used in many countries. You MUST verify for Syria.

Common Legal Bands for Wireless Microphones:

| Band Designation | Frequency Range | Common Use Region |

|------------------|-----------------|-------------------|

| G50 | 470-548 MHz | Europe (CEPT countries) |

| G54 | 518-542 MHz | Europe |

| J53 | 520-544 MHz | Europe |

| Band 7 | 506-530 MHz | Europe |

| Band 8 | 578-602 MHz | Europe |

| A1 | 516-558 MHz | Europe |

| K3E | 606-630 MHz | Europe |

Most Likely for Syria:

Based on regional patterns, Syria may follow European standards (ERC 70-03):

Probable Legal Bands:
  • 518-558 MHz (Common in CEPT countries)
  • 606-614 MHz (Secondary option)
  • 470-518 MHz (Possible, but often used by TV broadcasts)
Power Levels:
  • Typically: 10-50 mW EIRP (Effective Isotropic Radiated Power)
  • Wireless mics usually: 10-20 mW
  • In-ear monitors: 50 mW
